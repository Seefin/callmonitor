Rustic Bread

This bread is called rustic as it relies mostly upon yeast action to provide flavour, and has no chemical helpers like sugar or honey in it. It makes about 2 medium sized boules, or 1-and-a-bit loaves.

This dough is more technically an enriched dough due to the addition of olive oil.

Good with frseh butter and herbs, or as a chunky base to a grilled cheese.

Ingredients

  • Flour (Strong) 500g
  • Salt (table) 2tsp
  • Yeast (Active Dry) 7g (~34\frac{3}{4}43​tsp)
  • Water (warm) 300mL
  • Olive Oil 3tbsp
  • Flour (Wholemeal) 1 handful (For Dusting)
  • Salt (Flakey) 1 pinch (For Garnish)

Method

  1. Mix the strong flour and 23\frac{2}{3}32​ of the warm water in a bowl until just incorporated and leave to sit for 20 minutes - this is the autolyse step, and it allows for better gluten structure in the baked bread.
  2. Mix the yeast and the other 13\frac{1}{3}31​ the water in a small bowl, and leave to sit for 5 minutes, until the liquid has gone pale brown and the yeast has dissolved.
    • There should be some froth on top of the water, and a strong smell. If both of these indicators are absent, you yeast may be dead - Active dry yeast is shelf-stable in a pantry for up to a year, and up to two years in a fridge.
  3. Add the yeast-and-water mixture and the olive oil to the autolysed dough after the 20 minutes are up, and mix well to combine and form a rough dough.
  4. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured suface and sprinkle the salt over the top of the dough.
  5. Knead the dough, incorporating the table salt as you go.
  6. Once the dough is smooth (~20 minutes), place in a lightly oiled bowl to prove - either in an oven with the light on for ~40 - 60 minutes, or overnight in a refridgerator.
  7. When ready to bake, preheat oven to 220∘^{\circ}∘C conventional, 200∘^{\circ}∘C fan-forced, or gas-mark 7.
  8. Knock back the dough, and form into a bowl or a loaf, and place in a loaf pan or on a tray. Prove for another 60 minutes or until doubled in size.
  9. Dust the loaf with flour and flakey salt, and score with a sharp blade (preferably a lame).
    • Scoring the loaf creates weak points where the dough can expand - this prevents unsighlty 'growths' on your loaf!
  10. Bake for 25 - 30 minutes until golden brown and hollow soubding when tapped. You will want to bake in the middle of the oven if at all possible.
  11. Cool completely on a wire rack before eating, to allow for proper crust formation.

Notes

  • The crust on this can be a little thick for sandwich bread, so if using for that, bake in a loaf pan.
  • You'll want the oven to rise to and maintain heat reliably - chuck a pizza stone or some fire bricks as low as possible in the oven before baking to provide thermal mass and smooth out temprature fluctations experienced by the bread.
