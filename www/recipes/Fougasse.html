Fougasse (Sponge Method)

Following the LCB method from work, makes 2 fougasse, focassia or stromboi. This method is best if you have a consistent oven.

Uses fresh cake yeast. If this is unavailable (Has Moore Wilson's closed?), use the below table to convert.

Fresh Yeast Instant Yeast Active Dry Yeast
1gram 13\frac{1}{3}31​gram 12\frac{1}{2}21​gram

Ingredients

Sponge

  • Flour (strong) 225g
  • Water (Warm) 175g
  • Yeast 2.5g (~1tsp)

Dough

  • Flour (strong) 575g
  • Water 400g
  • Yeast 2.5g (~1tsp)

Toppings

  • Salt (Crunchy) 15g
  • Olive Oil 30g (Best use fresh)

Tapenade

  • Olive Oil 50mL
  • Chopped Olives 100g
  • Basil Leaves 20g

Method

  1. Make the sponge, and prove it for 40 minutes
    1. In a mixing bowl, dissolve yeast in water.
    2. Add flour and mix until a smooth paste forms
    3. Prove in a warm place (usually an oven with just the light on) for 40 minutes. It will not double in size, but will become airy and bubbly.
  2. Stir down the sponge, and add the rest of the dough ingredients.
  3. Turn out onto a floured surface, and knead until a smooth dough forms, and gluten is fully developed.
    • As you knead, the dough will become smoother, and the lumps will vanish. Gluten is fully developed when the dough is smooth, and, after resting, passes the windowpane test.
  4. Seperate into two equal pieces using scale and bench knife.
  5. Place into two clean, lightly floured, bowls and prove until doubled in volume (~40 minutes in an oven with the light on and nothing else)
  6. Oil a tray (~2 - 3 tbsp), and stretch the dough into a rectangle on the tray.
    1. Place the dough on the tray
    2. Stretch it into a rectangle shape that fills the whole tray/
      • If the dough doesn't take up the whole tray, loosely cover and leave for five minutes, then try again; the gluten is tightening as you work it, preventing the dough from stretching.
    3. You will need to re-stretch a few times over the course of ~30 minutes to get it to stay shaped; this is normal.
  7. Drizzle with more oil, dimple with your fingers, and prove again until doubled in size (~40 minutes).
    • If making liguarian foccacia, pour a 1:16 brine over the dough, filling in the dimples before proving.
  8. While dough is proving, make the tapenade (if using)
    1. Blend oil, basil, and olives until desired consistency is reached.
  9. If making focaccia, bake as is on the tray for 20 minutes at 200∘^{\circ}∘C, or until light golden in colour.
  10. If making stromboi, ignore steps 6 and 7. Instead:
    1. Roll dough into a long, narrow, rectangle.
    2. Spread tapenade on rectangle.
    3. Roll into a ring, and place on a baking tray to prove until doubled in size (~40 minutes).
    4. Bake for 20 minutes at 200∘^{\circ}∘C, until light golden in colour.

Notes

  • Samin Nostrat makes a similar, foccacia based on a recipe from Liguria.
    • This focaccia does not use a sponge starter, and uses honey in the dough, adding sugar for the yeast to feed on and deepening the golden colour when baked.
    • The intial proof in the bowl is done at room temprature overnight, allowing a smaller (by volume) amount of yeast to provide appropriate leavening.
    • This focaccia is more traditional, and is therefore not flavoured with any herbs or garnishes except salt.
  • To add rosemary, sage, thyme or other fresh herbs, mix them into the dough before kneading at step 3.
